Delicious Biryani Recipe

Enjoy the rich and aromatic flavors of traditional biryani made with perfectly spiced rice, meat, and herbs.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ cups basmati rice
  • 500g chicken (or mutton, beef, or vegetables)
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Whole spices (1 cinnamon stick, 3-4 cloves, 3-4 cardamom pods, 1 bay leaf)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves for garnish
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons fried onions (optional)
  • You can watch this video for your reference.

Instructions

  1. Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, heat the ghee and oil over medium heat. Add the whole spices (cinnamon, cloves, cardamom, bay leaf) and sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown. Remove half of the onions and set them aside for garnishing later.
  4. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute. Then, add the tomatoes and cook until soft and oil begins to separate from the masala.
  5. Add the chicken (or your choice of protein) and cook until browned on all sides.
  6. Add yogurt, green chilies, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook until the chicken is tender and the oil separates from the masala.
  7. In a separate large pot, bring 2 cups of water to a boil and add the soaked rice. Cook the rice until it's 70% done (the rice should be firm, not fully cooked). Drain the water and set aside.
  8. Layer the partially cooked rice over the cooked chicken mixture. Cover the pot tightly and cook on low heat (using the "Dum" method) for 20-25 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and the flavors are blended together.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves, mint leaves, and fried onions (optional). Serve hot with raita, salad, or chutney.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Biryani!
